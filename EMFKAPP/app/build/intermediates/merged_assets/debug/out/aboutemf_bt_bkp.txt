		<p><a href="https://i0.wp.com/www.emergencymedicinekenya.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/emk_abt_head.png" target="_blank"><img class="aligncenter wp-image-923 size-full" src="aboutemf_bt2_files/emk_abt_head.png" alt="emk_abt_head" srcset="https://i1.wp.com/www.emergencymedicinekenya.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/emk_abt_head.png?w=556 556w, https://i2.wp.com/www.emergencymedicinekenya.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/emk_abt_head.png?resize=300%2C53 300w" sizes="(max-width: 556px) 100vw, 556px" width="556" height="98"></a></p>
<p><strong>Emergency Medicine Kenya Foundation (EMKF) is a non-profit 
organisation founded in January 2015 dedicated to the development of 
emergency care in Kenya (and other resource-limited settings) through 
education and research.</strong></p>
<p>It&nbsp;is part of the Free Open Access to Medical Education (FOAMed)
 movement which is &nbsp;“a globally accessible crowd-sourced 
educational adjunct providing inline (contextual) and offline 
(asynchronous) content to augment traditional educational principles. 
FOAM is medical education for anyone, anywhere, anytime.”</p>
<p><strong>OUR VALUES</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Helping identify evidence-based ways to save lives in medical emergencies in Kenya (and other resource-limited settings)</li>
<li>Promoting resource appropriate clinical innovations that improve 
efficacy and contribute to economic benefits in delivering emergency 
health care in Kenya</li>
<li>Improving patient experience and outcome across emergency care systems in Kenya (and other resource-limited settings)</li>
<li>Developing practical research, education and promotion that raises 
public awareness of Emergency Medicine and fosters advancements in 
emergency care in Kenya (and other resource-limited settings)</li>
<li>Supporting local and international researchers identify and undertake appropriate and relevant emergency care research in Kenya</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>OUR VISION</strong> is to develop career emergency care 
providers in Kenya and promote local research that improves the outcomes
 for emergency care patients in Kenya (and other resource-limited 
settings).</p>
<p><strong>OUR MISSION</strong> is to achieve this through education, 
research, promotions, mentoring and building capacity for emergency care
 development in Kenya.</p>

 African Federation for Emergency Medicine (AFEM), founded in November 
2009, represents a broad coalition of national societies, organisations,
 and individuals from over 40 countries.&nbsp; AFEM’s membership is 
comprised of individuals with an interest in African emergency care, 
including non-clinicians, medics, nurses, and physicians. AFEM is all 
about promoting and expanding Emergency Care, from many angles, across 
the entire African continent. Based on priorities identified in regional
 consensus meetings, AFEM provides data to inform policy-making; offers 
open-access emergency care training curricula for nurses, midlevel 
providers, and specialists; coordinates continuing education programs 
for healthcare providers in emergency care; and much more.</p>
